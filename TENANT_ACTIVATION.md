# TENANT_ACTIVATION_PLAYBOOK.md — Site Revisions After Signing a Contractor Partner

**Use:** Apply this playbook to a Compass Camper LLC site immediately AFTER an exclusive partnership agreement is signed with a contractor. These revisions safely upgrade the site from "marketing service seeking partners" to "marketing service with established partner," improving trust signals and conversion rates without crossing compliance lines.

**Trigger:** A signed written agreement between Compass Camper LLC and a licensed contractor in the target market, including TCPA indemnity, additional-insured status, license verification, warranty pass-through, and 10-day consent revocation honor.

**Last updated:** May 2026
**Companion document:** COMPLIANCE_STANDARDS.md (the pre-tenant baseline)

---

## Activation Principles

When a tenant contractor is signed, these things become factually true:
1. You have a partner contractor (singular)
2. That contractor has documented credentials (license, insurance, warranty)
3. That contractor performs the actual work
4. Real customer reviews will accumulate over time

The site can now reflect these new truths. But it remains a marketing service operated by Compass Camper LLC. The disclosure strip, footer disclaimer, and core operating-entity language never change.

**What stays the same forever:**
- Header disclosure strip ("not a licensed contractor")
- Footer legal disclaimer
- Compass Camper LLC as the operating entity
- TCPA consent language on forms
- Privacy and Terms pages
- Schema entity (ProfessionalService, not contractor category)

**What changes when a tenant is signed:** Body copy, trust signals, reviews, schema enhancements, and the "About" page details.

---

## Pre-Activation Verification (Before Touching The Site)

Before making any of these site changes, verify:

- [ ] Written, executed contractor partnership agreement on file
- [ ] Contractor's current license number documented and verified with state licensing board
- [ ] Contractor's general liability insurance certificate on file (Compass Camper LLC listed as additional insured)
- [ ] Workmanship warranty terms documented in the agreement
- [ ] TCPA indemnity clause in agreement
- [ ] Lead delivery process agreed (Call Rail forwarding, form lead handoff method, response time SLA)
- [ ] Compass Camper LLC carries E&O $1M/$2M and cyber $1M
- [ ] TrustedForm or Jornaya integrated on all site forms
- [ ] DBA registered in both Oregon and target state

If any of these are not in place, do not apply tenant activation. The compliance risk increases significantly the moment the site claims a partnership.

## Language Activations (What Becomes Allowed)

Once a tenant is signed, these become factually defensible:

### Now allowed (singular tenant relationship)

- "Our partner contractor"
- "Our trusted [SERVICE] partner"
- "[Tenant brand name] is our exclusive [city] [SERVICE] partner"
- "We've partnered with [tenant name], a licensed [city] [SERVICE] contractor"
- "Our partner contractor handles all [SERVICE] work"

### Still off-limits (until you have multiple tenants OR documented vetting)

- "Our network" (implies plural, you have one)
- "Our vetted partners" (implies a documented vetting process; only allowed if you actually have a documented written vetting checklist applied to each tenant)
- "Top contractors in [city]" (implies ranking against others)
- "Hand-selected" (vague claim, hard to defend)

If you sign multiple contractors over time (one per market or multiple per market), you can pluralize. But until then, keep it singular.

### Documented vetting

If you want to use the word "vetted," it requires a documented process. Acceptable vetting criteria:
- License verification with state board
- Insurance certificate review
- BBB rating check (no unresolved complaints)
- Reference check with 3+ recent customers
- Warranty terms review
- Background check on principals (optional, raises bar)

If you document and execute this process for each tenant, "vetted" becomes defensible. Keep the documentation on file in case of FTC inquiry.

## Site Revisions to Apply (Standardized)

The following changes should be made when a tenant is signed. These are the standardized revisions that work across foundation repair, roofing, HVAC, plumbing, or any service vertical.

### Revision 1: Header disclosure strip update

**Pre-tenant copy (matches the shortened 2026-07-11 template in COMPLIANCE_STANDARDS.md):**
> "A marketing service, not a licensed contractor. [SERVICE] work is performed by independent licensed local contractors."

**Post-tenant copy:**
> "A marketing service, not a licensed contractor. [SERVICE] work is performed by our partner [TENANT_BRAND], License #[LICENSE_NUMBER]."

The disclosure stays. The framing updates to name the tenant.

### Revision 2: Footer tagline update

**Pre-tenant copy:**
> "Connecting [CITY]-area homeowners with licensed, insured [SERVICE] contractors. Fast response. Honest assessments."

**Post-tenant copy:**
> "Connecting [CITY]-area homeowners with [TENANT_BRAND], our trusted local [SERVICE] partner. Fast response. Honest assessments."

### Revision 3: Footer legal disclaimer (NO CHANGE)

Keep current text. The legal disclaimer is correct as-is.

### Revision 4: Homepage hero sub-headline

**Pre-tenant copy:**
> "Get a free, no-obligation foundation inspection from a licensed local contractor today."

**Post-tenant copy:**
> "Get a free, no-obligation [SERVICE] inspection from our partner contractor, [TENANT_BRAND]."

### Revision 5: "Why Trust Us" section block

The four trust pillars get upgrades. Specifically the "Licensed and Insured" pillar:

**Pre-tenant copy:**
> "Licensed and Insured Contractors: Every contractor you're matched with is required to carry current [STATE] contractor licensing and general liability insurance."

**Post-tenant copy:**
> "Licensed and Insured: Our partner contractor [TENANT_BRAND] holds current [STATE] contractor license #[LICENSE_NUMBER] and carries general liability insurance. License and insurance documentation is available upon request."

This is a major credibility upgrade because it names a real contractor and a real license number. It's also fully verifiable, so it's defensible.

### Revision 6: "How It Works" section

The 4-step process can now reference the tenant by name:

**Step 1 update:**
> "Free Inspection: A licensed [SERVICE] specialist from [TENANT_BRAND] visits your home and identifies every issue."

**Step 2 update:**
> "Written Estimate: [TENANT_BRAND] provides a detailed, itemized written estimate before any work begins."

**Step 3 update:**
> "Repair: [TENANT_BRAND] completes the work, protects your landscaping, and treats your home with respect."

**Step 4 update:**
> "Warranty: Qualifying repairs are backed by [TENANT_BRAND]'s transferable warranty. Documentation is provided directly by [TENANT_BRAND] for your records."

### Revision 7: About page activation

The About page gets a major upgrade. Add a new section: "About Our Partner Contractor."

**New section template:**

```
## About Our Partner Contractor: [TENANT_BRAND]

[TENANT_BRAND] is our exclusive [SERVICE] partner serving [CITY] and surrounding suburbs.

**Credentials:**
- Licensed in [STATE]: License #[LICENSE_NUMBER]
- General liability insurance: $[COVERAGE_AMOUNT] (certificate available upon request)
- BBB rating: [RATING]
- Workmanship warranty: [WARRANTY_TERMS]

[TENANT_BRAND] performs all [SERVICE] work for homeowners matched through this service. Compass Camper LLC operates the website and the lead matching service; [TENANT_BRAND] performs the actual repairs.

When you submit an inspection request through this site, your information goes directly to [TENANT_BRAND]. They will contact you to schedule your free inspection within [SLA_TIME].
```

Keep the existing Compass Camper LLC operating-entity section above this. The new section is additive, not replacing.

### Revision 8: Contractor name in service area pages

City pages can now mention the tenant. Add a small line near the top of each city page:

> "[TENANT_BRAND], our partner [SERVICE] contractor, serves homeowners throughout [CITY] and the surrounding area."

This naturally integrates the tenant into local SEO content without sacrificing accuracy.

### Revision 9: FAQ updates

Several FAQs become more specific now that a tenant is signed:

**"Who actually performs the foundation repair?"** (Add this if it's not already an FAQ)
> "All [SERVICE] work is performed by [TENANT_BRAND], our partner contractor. Compass Camper LLC operates this website and the matching service. [TENANT_BRAND] holds [STATE] contractor license #[LICENSE_NUMBER] and carries current general liability insurance."

**"How quickly can someone come out?"**
> "Once you submit an inspection request, [TENANT_BRAND] will contact you within [SLA_TIME] to schedule your free inspection."

**"Do you offer financing?"**
> "Financing availability is determined by [TENANT_BRAND]. They will discuss any available payment options during your free inspection."

**"Are estimates really free?"**
> "Yes. [TENANT_BRAND] provides free, no-obligation written estimates for every inspection."

### Revision 10: Schema updates

#### LocalBusiness/ProfessionalService schema (Compass Camper LLC)

The Compass Camper LLC schema stays as ProfessionalService. No change.

#### Add new Organization schema for the tenant

On the About page, add a second JSON-LD schema block for the tenant contractor:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Organization",
  "name": "[TENANT_BRAND]",
  "url": "[TENANT_WEBSITE_IF_ANY]",
  "telephone": "[TENANT_PHONE_OR_SHARED_TRACKING]",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "addressLocality": "[CITY]",
    "addressRegion": "[STATE]",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "description": "Licensed [SERVICE] contractor serving [CITY] and surrounding areas. [STATE] contractor license #[LICENSE_NUMBER]."
}
```

This is fully verifiable, defensible, and adds entity-level SEO signals.

#### Service schema upgrades

Service pages can now have a `provider` field pointing to the tenant:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Service",
  "name": "[SERVICE_NAME] in [CITY]",
  "serviceType": "[SERVICE_TYPE]",
  "provider": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "[TENANT_BRAND]"
  },
  "areaServed": "[CITY], [STATE]"
}
```

#### AggregateRating and Review schema (ONLY if real reviews exist)

After tenant signing AND after accumulating at least 10 real customer reviews from verifiable sources (Google reviews on the tenant's GBP, written customer testimonials with permission to publish, etc.), you can add:

```json
{
  "@type": "AggregateRating",
  "ratingValue": "[ACTUAL_AVERAGE]",
  "reviewCount": "[ACTUAL_COUNT]",
  "bestRating": "5",
  "worstRating": "1"
}
```

**Critical rules for review schema:**
- Every number must reflect real, verifiable reviews
- Source of reviews must be documented (where they came from)
- Reviews must be of the tenant contractor, not Compass Camper LLC
- If reviews are pulled from a third-party platform (Google, Yelp), the platform should be cited
- Get written permission from individual reviewers before naming them in a Review schema entry
- FTC Reviews Rule penalties (up to $53,088 per violation) make this critical

## Trust Element Additions (Optional, Recommended)

Once a tenant is signed, these site additions safely increase trust without compliance risk:

### Real before/after photo gallery

Photos of the tenant's actual completed work. Get written permission from homeowners before publishing photos of their property. Use captions like:
> "Foundation repair in [neighborhood], completed by [TENANT_BRAND] in [month/year]"

### Real customer testimonials

Once real customers have left reviews (or given written testimonial permission), publish 3-5 on the homepage and About page. Format:

> "[Quote from real customer]"
> — [First name] [Last initial], [Neighborhood], [Year]

Get written permission. Keep the signed permission form on file.

### Real certifications and credentials

If the tenant holds specific certifications (e.g., specific pier manufacturer certifications, BBB accreditation, industry association memberships), display these with proper attribution:
> "[TENANT_BRAND] is a certified installer of [Product]"
> "[TENANT_BRAND] is BBB accredited (A+ rating)"

Only display credentials the tenant actually holds. Document them in the partnership file.

### Local press features (if any)

If the tenant has been featured in local news, blogs, or industry publications, link to those features with proper attribution. Example:
> "[TENANT_BRAND] was featured in the [Publication] [Month Year] article on [Topic]: [Link]"

## Things That Never Change

Regardless of how many tenants are signed or how successful the site becomes, these elements stay forever:

1. **Compass Camper LLC operating entity disclosure** in header strip and footer
2. **TCPA consent language** on every form
3. **Privacy and Terms pages** with required disclosures
4. **Schema entity type** for Compass Camper LLC stays as ProfessionalService (not contractor)
5. **No fake reviews or invented testimonials** — only real, verifiable customer content
6. **No fake tenure claims** — Compass Camper LLC's actual operating history
7. **No fake certifications** — only verified credentials of the actual tenant

## When a Tenant Relationship Ends

If the tenant partnership ends (contract expires, dispute, tenant goes out of business), immediately:

1. Revert all body copy to pre-tenant language (per COMPLIANCE_STANDARDS.md)
2. Remove tenant-specific Organization schema
3. Remove tenant license numbers, certifications, and branded references
4. Remove tenant-specific testimonials and reviews from on-site display (though they can stay on the tenant's own GBP/Google reviews)
5. Update the header disclosure strip back to plural "licensed contractors" language
6. Pause any lead generation until a new tenant is signed OR have a documented process for honest handling of incoming leads (referral to a third party, honest "we're between partners" notice, etc.)

**Do not leave outdated tenant claims on the site after the partnership ends.** That becomes false advertising.

## Conversion Lift Expectations

Adding a real, named partner contractor with verifiable credentials typically increases site conversion by 30-60%. The biggest gains come from:

1. Named license number in the trust block
2. Tenant brand name in the About page section
3. Real photo gallery of completed work
4. 3-5 real customer testimonials with first names and neighborhoods

The lift is real because homeowners feel less uncertainty about who they're trusting with their home. Pre-tenant language is honest but creates more friction. Post-tenant language is just as honest AND more specific, which is what closes leads.

## Sample Tenant Contractor Agreement Outline

For the operator's reference. A full agreement should be drafted with an attorney, but this is the skeleton to provide them:

1. **Parties:** Compass Camper LLC (Oregon) and [Tenant LLC/Corp] (target state)
2. **Term:** Initial 12-month term, auto-renewal with 30-day termination notice
3. **Exclusivity:** Compass Camper LLC will not provide leads from [SITE_BRAND] to other contractors in [target geography]
4. **Lead delivery:** Method (Call Rail forwarding, form lead emails, etc.), response time SLA
5. **Compensation:** Flat monthly fee, or per-lead fee, or per-closed-job fee — defined in writing
6. **Quality standards:** Tenant maintains license, insurance, BBB rating, written warranties
7. **TCPA indemnity:** Tenant indemnifies Compass Camper LLC for any TCPA claim arising from tenant's contact with leads
8. **Additional insured:** Compass Camper LLC listed as additional insured on tenant's GL
9. **Customer data:** Tenant uses lead data only for inspection follow-up, doesn't sell or transfer
10. **10-day consent revocation:** Tenant honors any STOP/opt-out within 10 days
11. **Marketing claims:** Tenant approves all marketing copy that names them; Compass Camper LLC approves any marketing copy where tenant references Compass Camper LLC
12. **Termination:** Conditions, notice period, post-termination obligations
13. **Governing law:** Oregon
14. **Dispute resolution:** Arbitration clause optional, otherwise Oregon courts
