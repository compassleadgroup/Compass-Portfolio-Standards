# Reference-exemplar registry

The site-design skill uses this. It answers two questions for a build: what treatment to aim for in this niche, and where to find concrete exemplars to build against. The point is to give the build real visuals to reason from, then translate them onto our own tokens. Prose alone lands on the generic look; a concrete exemplar, adapted, does not.

## How to use it

1. Find the niche family below and read its treatment target.
2. Fetch three to five concrete exemplars from the sources named. In a build session with browse, screenshot each live page into a local build-scratch references folder. Without browse, use screenshots the operator pastes.
3. Build each section against the matching exemplar, under the translate-not-copy rules.

## Translate, do not copy (the rules that make this safe and non-generic)

- Apply the treatment that makes sense, not one that matches an exemplar one to one. Do not clone a footer or hero because one exemplar had it.
- Keep the composition and the restraint. That is what you are borrowing: the structure, the rhythm, the discipline.
- Replace everything brand-specific. Their color, their wordmark, their photography, their copy, all become this site's own tokens. Nothing brand-specific transfers.
- The exemplars are a guideline the build references while it works, not a source to reproduce.

Exemplar screenshots are private build references. Never deploy them, never copy their assets into the live site, and keep them out of the shipped repo (build scratch or gitignored). This is reasoning material, not content.

## Cross-category sourcing (the free substitute for a paid inspiration tool)

Lift the ambition from a premium adjacent category, then reskin to the niche. A local home-service site is not a startup, so borrow the discipline of premium sectors and marry it to the subject's real regional texture.

Hard rule: never source from other lead-gen or rank-and-rent sites in the niche. Those are wearing the exact look we are escaping. Borrowing from them re-creates the footprint.

## Durable places to find exemplars

Live sites change and screenshots go stale, so search fresh at build time rather than trusting a fixed URL. Good starting points:

- General design galleries that have lasted: Awwwards, SiteInspire, Land-book, Httpster, Godly, Minimal Gallery. Filter by the borrow-from category, not by "landing page."
- The premium adjacent category itself: search the category name plus "website," then take the two or three that look studio-made.
- The subject's authentic texture: trade associations, equipment makers, and government or university pages in the niche carry real domain detail (a soil-survey map, a snow-load table, a wind-rose) that no generic template has. Borrow the texture, not the layout.

Generalized from the portfolio's real design records (the design-distinctiveness doctrine in compass-kb, and each site's design record). Change a lane against the sibling site; these are the family direction, not a fixed skin.
