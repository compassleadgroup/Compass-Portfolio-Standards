# Rank-and-Rent Reference Doc (2026)

Compressed operator reference. Home services lead gen. US market.

---

## VALUATION

**Two frameworks:**

1. **Rent pricing:** monthly leads × close rate × avg ticket × contractor margin × 10% = monthly rent. Example: 25 leads × 15% × $9,000 × 40% × 10% = $1,350/mo rent.
2. **Sale price:** monthly net profit × multiple (3–12 mo trailing average).

**2026 sale multiples (× monthly profit):**

| Site type | Multiple | Venue |
|---|---|---|
| Single-city, single-tenant, flat rent, <12mo | 18–24× | Flippa |
| Single-city, multi-tenant or PPL, 12–24mo | 24–30× | Empire Flippers (needs $1k+/mo) |
| Multi-city portfolio, 2+yr | 28–34× | EF / Quiet Light |
| Established directory + database, 5+yr | 34–40×+ | Quiet Light / private |

**Multiplier drivers:**
- Discounts: single tenant (−15 to −30%), GBP-dependent (−10 to −20%), pure organic only (−10 to −15%), recent algo volatility (−15%), no SOPs (−5 to −15%), owner does >5hr/wk (−5 to −10%)
- Premiums: diversified traffic (+5 to +15%), multi-tenant/PPL (+10%), 2+yr stable (+5 to +10%), trademarked brand + database (+10 to +20%), 5+yr age (+10%)

**Real comps:**
- EF #86318: Canadian home services, $1,053/mo profit, $29,486 asking = **28×**
- EF #92725: Family resource (incl. lead gen), $4,664/mo, $172,584 = 37×
- Flippa #12071684: EU home construction lead platform, €3,750/mo, ~€135k = ~36×

**Buyer type matters:**
- Selling to renting contractor: 12–24× (their BATNA = stop paying)
- Selling to operator/investor: 24–32× (broker-driven)
- Selling to franchise/association: 30–40× (rare, high-value)

**Critical rule:** EF explicitly discounts direct rank-and-rent sites because tenants may quit when ownership transfers. Build ASSET-transferable: own domain, content, GBP, call tracking, phone number, leads.

| Roofing | $50–$120 | $100–$350 | $200–$500 | $5k–$50k |
| Foundation | $30–$75 | $55–$150 | ~$99 | $5k–$30k |
| Water damage | $50–$300 | $150–$700 | $400–$750 | $2.5k–$25k |

Foundation repair Tulsa benchmark = $75/lead (LeadsForward).
Inquirly avg winning CPL: plumbing $60, HVAC $90, roofing $90.
Service Direct avg HVAC CPL: $115–$153. Water damage national avg: $542.

**Flat rent ranges:**
- Tier 3 city, low ticket: $250–$600/mo
- Tier 2 city, mid ticket: $500–$1,500/mo
- Tier 2 city, high ticket: $1,000–$3,000/mo
- Tier 1 city, high ticket: $2,000–$5,000/mo
- Specialty/restoration major metro: $3k–$10k/mo

**Revenue share:**
- Typical: 5–10% of closed-job revenue
- Ippei case: Naperville commercial flat roofing = $52k/mo at 10% rev share on $67,640 lead-driven revenue
- Verification: read-only CRM access (ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro/Jobber) OR CallRail-attributed monthly reconciliation
- Dispute clause: >2 under-reports in 6mo terminates contract

**Hybrid models (2026 sweet spot):**
- Base + per-lead: $300/mo + $50/qualified call
- Base + rev share: $500/mo + 5% closed jobs

**Profitability ranking:**
- Highest upside: rev share with great closer
- Most common in 2026: pay-per-lead
- Easiest to sell at exit: flat rent w/ long tenant
- Best risk/reward: hybrid base + per-lead

**Contract structure operators use:**
- Month-to-month, 30-day notice OR 90-day initial then MTM
- First month free or half-priced as sales tool
- 6-month commit w/ quarterly rate escalators for high-value sites

## SOURCING TENANT CONTRACTORS

**The winning pitch = "leads-in-hand":** Generate 2–10 leads first. Call contractor: "I have X leads from my Tulsa foundation site this week. Free trial for 2 weeks. If real, exclusive at $X/lead or $Y/mo."

**Outbound that works:**
- Cold call contractors on **page 2 of Google** for target query (already SEO-aware, not dominating)
- Cold call HomeAdvisor/Angi/Thumbtack contractors (pre-educated on lead economics, pitch them exclusive vs. shared)
- LinkedIn outreach to home-service owners with 5–20 employees
- Direct mail to associations (NRCA, ACCA, ASHI)

**Inbound:**
- "Are you a contractor? Partner with us" CTA in footer. ~1–2% of organic visitors are contractor scouts.
- Local FB groups, post case studies, never pitch

**Marketplaces / brokers:**
- Empire Flippers ($1k+/mo profit floor, premium)
- Flippa (volume, lower vetting)
- Quiet Light ($100k+ deals)
- Motion Invest, Investors Club
- LeadsForward.com (only dedicated rank-and-rent marketplace; sample: $5,400 for 3-leads/mo OKC foundation site)

**No dedicated tenant marketplace exists.** Private deals via Job Killing/Ippei FB group, Digital Storefronts, Local Marketing Vault, Semantic Mastery.

**Pitches that convert first-timers:**
1. Free trial (2–4 leads free, then paid)
2. Risk reversal ("only pay for leads that become quotes for 30 days")
3. Competitor leverage ("their competitor is buying from me")
4. Replace Angi spend (position vs. existing pay-for-shared-lead budget)

**Education gap:** Don't explain SEO. Show #1 ranking screenshot + 5 CallRail recordings. Proof > explanation.

## TOP REASONS DEALS DIE

| Failure mode | % of failures | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Lead quality disputes | ~40% | Written qualified-lead definition, 48hr dispute window, 15% dispute cap, recordings/screenshots required |
| Volume volatility (algo, GBP suspensions) | ~25% | Never guarantee volume; "no leads = no rent" or rebate clauses; diversified traffic |
| Contractor outgrows leads | ~15% | First-right-of-refusal clause: 14 days to find sub-partner |
| Contractor goes out of business | ~10% | Operator owns phone number + forwarding; backup contractor identified |
| Operator-side (GBP suspension, payment) | ~10% | Don't use fake addresses; ACH auto-debit via Stripe/QuickBooks |

**Trust failures (rev share specific):** Without CRM access, contractors under-report closes. Required: read-only CRM access OR forced call-tracking attribution + monthly reconciliation.

**Legal traps:**
- FTC one-to-one consent rule (2024): exclusive single-tenant delivery has minimal exposure; multi-buyer lead resale requires TrustedForm certificates ($0.10–$0.30/lead)
- State licensing for "lead-gen matchmakers" in ~12 states (TX, CA, NV, MD, AZ etc.) — check before launch
- Truth-in-advertising: footer disclosure required ("marketing platform that connects homeowners with licensed contractors")

**Operator contract must-haves:**
1. Operator owns: domain, content, GBP, call tracking, number, all leads
2. Tenant: limited license to receive forwarded leads only
3. 30-day termination, no cause
4. 48hr lead dispute window
5. Disputes >15% review trigger, >25% renegotiate
6. Rev-share = mandatory CRM access OR call-tracking attribution
7. Tenant indemnifies operator for contractor malpractice
8. 24-month non-circumvent post-termination

## INDUSTRY STATE 2026

**Healthy but evolving.** GBP-spam version mostly dead (mass suspensions 2024–2025). Website-based, organic, multi-tenant version stronger than ever. Rising paid ad costs make organic leads more valuable.

**Paid ad cost benchmarks:**
- Overall Google Ads CPC: $5.26 (2025, +12.88% YoY) - WordStream
- Home & Home Improvement: $7.85 avg CPC
- HVAC keywords: $32.77 projected 2025 (up from $29.03 in 2024) - WebFX

**AI search impact:**
- AI Overviews on ~7% of local queries (WordStream "34 AI Overviews Stats" 2025)
- Whitespark Q2 2025 study (540 queries, 6 cities): AIOs on 68% of local business queries overall but only 15% of simple transactional ("nail salon near me"), 92% of informational, 97% of hybrid intent
- Semrush 10M+ keyword study: AIOs appeared on 6.49% of queries Jan 2025, peaked 24.61% July, stabilized ~15.69% Nov
- Informational pages lose 30–60% of clicks. Commercial "near me" intent largely intact.
- ChatGPT: 800M WAU (Oct 2025 TechCrunch/Altman Dev Day), 900M WAU Feb 2026 (DemandSage)
- Perplexity: 780M queries/mo, 30M/day (June 2025 TechCrunch, Srinivas at Bloomberg)
- **Strategic shift:** move site center of gravity from informational blog → transactional commercial pages

**LSA impact:**
- Oct 2025 Google consolidated Google Guaranteed/Screened into "Google Verified" badge; mandatory GBP verification for LSA
- LSA now in Maps, voice search, AI Overviews — above map pack and organic
- Tenant contractors compare your $50 lead to LSA's $30 lead; PPL pricing compressed at low end
- Counter-pitch: your leads are exclusive, LSA is shared

**Rising niches:** foundation repair, EV charger install, generators, spray foam, mold remediation, crawl space encapsulation, solar repair/maintenance, storm restoration

**Dying/saturated:** general handyman, carpet cleaning, garage door (LSA-locked), locksmith (GBP scrutiny), tier-1 city general plumbing

- **Dan Klein** — Job Killing co-creator, more hands-off now, weekly Zoom Q&A

- **Matt Diggity** — Definitive Rank and Rent Guide, Chiang Mai SEO Conference

**Operator scale tiers:**
- Year 1: 1–3 sites, $500–$2k/mo
- Year 2–3: 5–15 sites, $5k–$20k/mo
- Year 3+: 20–100+ sites, $30k–$200k/mo
- Outliers: 100–800+ domains, multi-six-figure monthly

## CAVEATS

- Operator income claims (Ippei, Dooley, Van Der Veer) are self-reported, unaudited
- Sale multiples come from listing prices; actual sold prices typically 5–15% below asking
- AI Overview prevalence is a moving target, not a stable equilibrium
- Independent sources (Elevation Labs Consulting) advise against new GBP-based rank-and-rent; operator-affiliated sources say it's healthier than ever. Both true: GBP-spam dying, website-based thriving.
- Pre-launch attorney review ($500–$1k) on tenant contract is not optional, especially for state-licensing exposure
