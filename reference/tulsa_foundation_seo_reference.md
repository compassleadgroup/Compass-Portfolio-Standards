## 3. Tulsa-Specific Content Angles

### Soil (THE single best topical authority play)
- Tulsa County: Pennsylvanian-age shale bedrock + red clay rich in **montmorillonite** (smectite group)
- Swells **10-15%** in volume when wet
- Can exert forces up to **5,500 psf** — enough to crack concrete
- ~235 dry days/year + heavy spring rain = swell/shrink cycle = peak inbound May-Sept

### Earthquakes (use carefully)
- USGS: "900-fold increase in quakes since 2008" (Scientific American)
- Driven by wastewater disposal injection (NOT fracking itself, per USGS)
- Sharply declined since 2016 regulations
- Homeowners still associate cracks with quakes — **"Did the earthquake crack my foundation?"** is an unowned content gap

### Older housing stock (foundation problems concentrated here)
- Maple Ridge: 1910s-1920s mansions (National Register)
- Brookside: 1920s + 1950s
- Florence Park: 1930s-1950s bungalows
- Lortondale: 1950s mid-century
- Many are pier-and-beam — matches early ranking signal

### Insurance
- Standard OK homeowners insurance does NOT cover soil-movement foundation damage
- Almost no Tulsa-specific page owns this query

### Licensing
- Oklahoma does NOT require state general-contractor license (Oklahoma Construction Industries Board)
- Trust signals matter more than credentials

## 9. Rank-and-Rent Risks (this niche)

1. **GBP suspension** if address looks virtual. Mitigation: tenant's verified address from day one, or organic-only.
2. **Fake review enforcement** — never buy. Harvest legitimately through tenant's job calls.
3. **Site reputation abuse policy** (Google, March 2024) — build site to look like a real business: real photos, real address, real phone, no keyword stuffing.
4. **Tenant lock-in** — write contracts month-to-month, 30-day notice, exclusive territory, non-compete preventing tenant from launching competing SEO site.
5. **Liability** — never imply site provides the service. Use "connecting Tulsa homeowners with vetted foundation experts" framing. Display tenant's license.
6. **Call tracking lock** — CallRail tracking numbers must be in your account, not tenant's, so they can't take them when contract ends.
