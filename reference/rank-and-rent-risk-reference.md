# Rank-and-Rent Risk Reference (2026)
**Context:** tulsafoundationexperts.com, operated by Compass Camper LLC (Oregon). Marketing service connecting Tulsa-area homeowners with licensed foundation contractors. Operator does not perform work.

---

## Top 3 actual risks (in order)
1. **GBP suspension/removal** of the "Tulsa Foundation Experts" name. Violates Google's names policy on its face.
2. **FTC Reviews Rule + state UDAP exposure** from "vetted," "lifetime warranty," fake/unsupported reviews. $53,088/violation.
3. **TCPA class action** flowing from the contractor partner's outbound calls. Lead-gen co-defendants are routine.

Oklahoma unlicensed-contractor risk is near-zero. CIB does not license foundation work; no state GC license exists.

---

## State Licensing & "Holding Out"

### Oklahoma (favorable)
- CIB licenses: Plumbing, Electrical, HVAC, Roofing (registration), Inspectors. **No foundation, no general contractor.**
- Source: https://oklahoma.gov/cib/frequently-asked-questions.html
- Tulsa: permit-pull registration only, no exam/bond.
- OKC: roofing permits required since Aug 1, 2025; residential roofing endorsement + exam required from July 1, 2026.

### "Holding out" states (risk if ads reach these residents)
- **CA BPC §7027, §7027.1, §7099**: advertising as a contractor triggers licensing law regardless of operations. §7099 CSLB citations up to $15K. §7099.10 allows phone disconnection. Misdemeanor. https://california.public.law/codes/business_and_professions_code_section_7027
- **FL Fla. Stat. §489.127**: bars advertising as contractor without license. 1st-degree misdemeanor; 3rd-degree felony during declared emergencies. https://www.flsenate.gov/laws/statutes/2024/489.127
- **AZ A.R.S. §32-1151**: Class 1 misdemeanor. AZ ROC mandates "Not a Licensed Contractor" disclaimer on all ads. https://roc.az.gov/news/contracting-arizona-heres-what-you-need-know
- **NC G.S. §87-13**: Class 2 misdemeanor for falsely suggesting licensure. Injunctive authority under §87-13.1. https://law.justia.com/codes/north-carolina/chapter-87/article-1/section-87-13/
- **TX**: no state GC license; TDLR regulates electrical/HVAC/plumbing. June 2023 sting filed 37 cases for ads lacking license info. Penalties up to $5K/violation.

### Required disclaimer pattern
Header + footer + form + IVR: "tulsafoundationexperts.com is a marketing service operated by Compass Camper LLC. We are not a licensed contractor and do not perform foundation repair. Services are performed by independent licensed contractors. Compass Camper LLC is not responsible for work performed by independent contractors."

## FTC Rules

### Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255, rev. June 2023)
Material connections must be clear/conspicuous. No hover-to-disclose.

### Consumer Reviews Rule (16 CFR Part 465, eff. Oct 21, 2024)
Prohibits: fake/AI-generated reviews, paid-sentiment reviews, undisclosed insider reviews, company-controlled "independent" review sites, review suppression via legal threats or selective display, fake social engagement.
- Penalty: **$53,088/violation** (Federal Register Doc. 2025-01361; OMB M-26-11 holds 2026 flat).
- First sweep: Dec 22, 2025 (10 warning letters).
- Source: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/08/federal-trade-commission-announces-final-rule-banning-fake-reviews-testimonials

### Bellwether case: *DOJ/FTC + IL AG + MN AG v. B.E.S.T. GDR LLC d/b/a Premium Home Service / Yosef Bernath* (N.D. Ill., May 12, 2026)
- Conduct: thousands of fake local listings since 2018, fabricated 5-star reviews using stolen photos, VoIP local numbers, Philippines call center, unlicensed sub dispatches. KARE 11 estimated $79M+ revenue.
- Charges: FTC Act §5, Reviews Rule, GLBA, IL Consumer Fraud Act, IL Uniform Deceptive Trade Practices Act.
- **CEO personally named.** Officer/owner liability is now standard in these complaints.
- Sources: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/05/ftc-illinois-take-action-stop-deceptive-conduct-company-created-thousands-business-listings-fake | https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/department-justice-files-complaint-against-best-gdr-llc-doing-business-premium-home-service | https://www.ag.state.mn.us/Office/Communications/2026/05/11_Premium-Home-Services.asp

### Other FTC actions
- **FTC v. Response Tree LLC / Derek Doherty** (Jan 2024): $7M suspended judgment, lifetime telemarketing ban. 50+ "consent farm" sites. https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/01/california-based-lead-generator-agrees-settlement-banning-it-making-or-assisting-others-making
- **FTC v. Rytr LLC**: settled Dec 2024, vacated Dec 22, 2025 under Trump AI Action Plan. Signal: FTC backs off neutral AI tools, still pursues businesses posting fake reviews.
- **Healthcare lead-gen warning letters** (Dec 10, 2024): 21 companies.

### "Vetted"/"lifetime warranty"/"free inspection" claims
Express §5 affirmations. "Vetted" without documented vetting process is deceptive. Acceptable substitute: "We require partner contractors to provide proof of Oklahoma CIB registration where applicable, current general liability insurance, and a written warranty on workmanship; specific warranty terms are set by the contractor in your contract."

## Google Business Profile + LSA

### GBP — "Tulsa Foundation Experts" name fails on its face
- Names policy: prohibits "Service or product information (e.g., 'Midas Auto Service Experts')" and "Location information." Source: https://support.google.com/business/answer/3038177
- Prohibited content: "Lead generation agents or companies" are ineligible for a Business Profile. Source: https://support.google.com/business/answer/7400114

### 2024-2026 suspension trends
- Google 2025 Maps Trust & Safety Report (Apr 16, 2026): "removed over 13 million fake Business Profiles" and "blocked or removed over 292 million policy-violating reviews" in 2025. Source: https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/maps/new-ways-were-protecting-businesses-on-maps/
- Sterling Sky 2024 data: 42% address-related, 28% name-related, 17% review-manipulation.
- Appeal times ~5 weeks. Source: https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-business-profile-suspensions-rise-appeals-are-delayed/542602/
- Common triggers: virtual/registered-agent address, keyword-stuffed name, SAB with displayed address, rapid edits, duplicate listings.

### LSA — non-contractor lead sellers banned
- "you may not sell the leads that you receive through your Local Services Ads."
- "All service providers and businesses acting on behalf of providers — such as agencies, lead generation, aggregators and lead management companies — must abide by the following policies."
- Source: https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/6245891
- Background checks via Pinkerton + Evident. $1M GL typical.
- Google Verified badge replaced Google Guaranteed Oct 20, 2025; consumer $2,000 money-back guarantee ended Nov 7, 2025. Any "Google Guaranteed" claim on site now is deceptive.

### Google enforcement litigation
- *Google v. Hu* (N.D. Cal., June 2023): 350+ fake profiles, 14,000+ fake reviews.
- *Google v. Pondit/BigBoostUp.com* + parallel Amazon action (Oct 2024).

## TCPA / Telemarketing

### Status of one-to-one consent rule
- Vacated: *Insurance Marketing Coalition v. FCC*, No. 24-10277 (11th Cir. Jan 24, 2025).
- FCC formally rescinded Aug 29, 2025.
- Multi-seller blanket consent legal again, but **prior express written consent still required** for autodialed/prerecorded calls/texts to wireless numbers.
- Consent revocation must be honored within 10 business days (FCC rule eff. Apr 11, 2025).

### Litigation volume
- WebRecon: 2,788 TCPA cases filed in 2024, up 67% from 2023.
- Q1 2025: 507 class actions, up 112% YoY. Source: https://natlawreview.com/article/tcpa-class-action-filings-explode-massive-final-numbers-are-2024-and-january-2025
- Statutory damages: $500-$1,500/call or text.

### Relevant settlements
- ***Ford v. Farha Roofing, LLC / Summa Media, LLC*** (W.D. Mo. 4:23-cv-00635): $250K final approval July 2025. Lead-gen vendor (Summa) co-defendant. Roofing text campaigns. Template for what plaintiffs will do to a Tulsa foundation lead-gen op.
- ***Williams v. PillPack*** (W.D. Wash.): $6.5M. Lead-gen got consent for itself, then routed call from PillPack whose name wasn't on the form. Precedent against generic consent.
- Median TCPA class settlement: $3.5-4.5M. Average $6.6M is skewed by outliers (National Grid $38.5M).

### State AG TCPA/TSR theory
- *PA AG v. Fluent, Inc.* (W.D. Pa., Nov 2022): lead-gen "substantial assistance" under TSR. Theory: lead seller liable for downstream telemarketing violations it knew or should have known about.

## Liability Structure

### Entity stack
- **Per-site or per-vertical operating LLC** owned by a **WY/DE holding LLC** for portfolios.
- **Avoid series LLCs.** Oregon has no series LLC statute. Untested outside real estate.
- Commercial registered agent (Northwest, Harbor Compliance). Not virtual office, not personal address as the public-facing address.
- **Never use registered agent address on the site or GBP.** Use a real (residential is fine) operating address, hidden on GBP via SAB config.

### Insurance
- **E&O / Professional Liability**: $1M/$2M. Hiscox, NEXT, Coalition.
- **Cyber**: $1M minimum. Mandatory given homeowner PII collection. Covers OK breach notification (24 O.S. §161 et seq.).
- **General Liability with media-liability endorsement**: $1M/$2M. Confirm advertising-injury endorsement; many GL carriers exclude.
- Contractor's GL does not cover the website operator.

### Contractor partner agreement clauses
- Contractor indemnifies Compass Camper for: work claims, TCPA/TSR claims arising from contractor calls, warranty claims, licensure complaints, contractor CRM breaches.
- Compass Camper indemnifies for unauthorized marketing copy + advertising-injury arising solely from website.
- Compass Camper named as additional insured on contractor GL.
- Contractor must provide TCPA-compliant consent at any contractor-initiated call (covers Fluent theory).

### TCPA consent capture
- TrustedForm (ActiveProspect) or Jornaya LeadiD on every form. Retain 5+ years.
- Consent language above the submit button (not popup, not fine print): name Compass Camper LLC + specific contractor partner, authorize calls/texts including autodialed, opt-out instructions.

### Tax / banking
- OK does not tax most marketing services (68 O.S. §1354 enumeration).
- Watch HI GET, NM gross receipts, SD, WV if expanding.
- Separate business bank account per LLC (basic veil rule).
- Contractor chargeback risk > consumer chargeback risk. Use net-30 ACH or pre-paid lead packs.

## Emerging 2026 Risks
- **CA Delete Act** (deletion mechanism eff. Aug 1, 2026): if sharing homeowner data with multiple contractors, may trigger data broker registration with CPPA. $200/day for failure to register.
- **AI content disclosure laws**: CA AB 2013, UT AI Policy Act, CO AI Act layered on top of Reviews Rule. AI-generated reviews still violate Reviews Rule post-Rytr (rule targets the deployer, not the tool).
- **Insurance carrier pushback** on third-party lead sources (Nationwide, Liberty Mutual, Travelers commercial) increasingly require contractors source from LSA/HomeAdvisor/Angi only.
- **Officer/owner personal liability** is now standard in AG complaints (Bernath in PHS).
- **Domain takedown** as equitable relief sought in PHS complaint.

## Action Plan

### 30 days
1. Plan for GBP loss. Either abandon the GBP using the keyword-stuffed name or transfer it to the contractor partner's real legal name (with consent), with you as manager.
2. Add unmissable disclaimer: header + footer + form + IVR.
3. Purge any review you can't trace to a real customer of the partner contractor. No AI testimonials. Clear-and-conspicuous disclosure on any owner/family/employee endorsement.
4. Install TrustedForm or Jornaya. Above-submit consent naming Compass Camper + partner contractor by name.
5. Sign contractor partner agreement with TCPA indemnity, additional-insured GL, license-verification covenant, warranty pass-through, 10-day revocation honor.
6. Verify partner contractor license status (n/a for pure foundation work in OK, but check if related plumbing/structural work involved).

### 90 days
7. Bind E&O $1M/$2M, cyber $1M, GL $1M/$2M with media endorsement.
8. Move site contact address to a real operating address. Kill the registered-agent address on public-facing materials.
9. If scaling: stand up WY or DE holding LLC over Compass Camper LLC. One site = one operating LLC.
10. Document a real contractor-vetting process (license screenshots, COI, BBB, complaints, performance log).
11. TCPA audit: DNC scrubbing (PossibleNOW or DNC.com), 5-year consent retention, 8 AM-9 PM local calling, OK Telemarketer Restriction Act compliance.
12. File DBA "Tulsa Foundation Experts" with OK SoS under Compass Camper LLC.

### 180 days
13. Diversify lead sources off Google: Microsoft Ads, direct mail, local sponsorships, referral program.
14. Quarterly compliance review: reviews, disclaimers, consent language, contractor license currency, insurance currency.
15. Pre-retain TCPA defense counsel (Troutman Amin, Squire Patton Boggs, or local OK firm) and UDAP counsel. $20K retainer is cheaper than $300K post-incident.
16. Five-alarm triggers (counsel within 72 hours): any state AG CID/subpoena, FTC §6(b) order or warning letter, TCPA class complaint naming Compass Camper or operator, contractor board cease-and-desist from any state, GBP suspension surviving two appeals.

### Decision pivots
- Partner unlicensed for any advertised work category → sever immediately.
- Scaling to 5+ sites across verticals → series LLC analysis becomes worthwhile.
- Want to run LSA → cannot under Compass Camper. Contractor must run LSA in their own name; Compass becomes their agency manager.
- CA/FL/AZ/NC traffic exceeds 10% of leads → state-specific landing pages with partner license number prominent, or geo-exclude.

## Caveats
- Not legal advice. Engage OK counsel for contractor agreement, FTC/UDAP-experienced counsel for advertising claims.

- Watch PHS docket. Preliminary injunction grant = copycat AG wave through 2026-27.
