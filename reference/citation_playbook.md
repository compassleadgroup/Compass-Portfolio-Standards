## Week 3: Content & Editorial Setup

### HARO / Connectively
**URL:** https://connectively.us/
**Time:** 30 minutes setup, then 60 min/week ongoing

**Setup:**
1. Sign up as a "Source" (not a journalist)
2. Set categories: "Business and Finance," "Lifestyle and Fitness," "General"
3. Add keywords to your email filter: "foundation," "home repair," "real estate," "homeowner," "Tulsa," "Oklahoma"
4. You'll get 3 emails per day with journalist queries

**Response strategy:**
- Open the emails 3x per day (morning, lunch, evening)
- Look for queries you can genuinely answer in 5 minutes
- Respond fast — journalists work on tight deadlines
- Use the template in the spreadsheet
- Track each response in your tracker

**Expected outcome:** 1-3 successful placements per month if consistent.

**URL:** https://medium.com/
**Time:** 30 min setup, 90 min per article

**Setup:**

## Week 4: Outreach Pitches

### Tulsa Real Estate Blog Pitches

**Research first:**
- Google "Tulsa real estate blog"
- Look for blogs that publish guest content
- 5 candidates I'd start with:
  1. Tulsa World Real Estate section
  2. TulsaHomes blog (if active)
  3. Realtor.com local Tulsa content
  4. Greater Tulsa Association of REALTORS member blogs
  5. Individual realtor blogs (use Google search)

**Pitch template (from your spreadsheet, refined):**

Subject: Guest article: How Tulsa's [current season] is affecting home foundations

Hi [Name],

I noticed you've covered [specific recent topic] on your blog. I run Tulsa Foundation Experts and I'm seeing a real pattern lately:

[Specific data point or anecdote about Tulsa foundation trends — be specific about what you're seeing in the market]

I'd like to write an 800-1,200 word article for your readers covering:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]  
- [Point 3]
- What homeowners should do about it

No payment needed — just a byline with a link back to my site. I can draft and send by [specific date].

Either way, appreciate the content you put out.

Anthony, Tulsa Foundation Experts
tulsafoundationexperts.com

**Tip:** Personalize the "noticed your recent post" line. Generic pitches get 0% response. Personalized pitches get 10-20%.

## When To Stop Building Citations

You're done with the citation/link building phase when:

1. You have 25-30 verified live citations
2. Search Console shows 100+ impressions per week (vs your current 18 in 7 days)
3. You're appearing in the top 30-40 positions for several queries
4. You've published 3-5 pieces of content on third-party sites

That typically takes 3-4 months of consistent work. After that, focus shifts to:
- Converting traffic to leads (you have form/phone live)
- Approaching contractors for tenant partnership
- Optimizing pages based on Search Console data

## Compliance Reminders

These are not optional:

1. **Never claim Compass Camper LLC is a foundation contractor.** Pick marketing/referral categories.
2. **Never solicit fake reviews.** Leave review sections empty until you have a tenant with real customers.
3. **Never check certification/licensing boxes** you can't back up with paperwork.
4. **Use exact NAP from your packet** on every directory. Inconsistency hurts SEO worse than not submitting.
5. **If a directory rejects you, accept it.** Don't resubmit with modified information. Update your tracker and move on.

## Realistic Expectations

**What this work will do (in 3-4 months):**
- Push you from 18 impressions/week to 200-500
- Move your average position from 67 to maybe 25-40
- Get you ranked on page 2-3 for "foundation repair Tulsa"
- Generate occasional form submissions (5-15/month)

**What this work will NOT do:**
- Put you on page 1 in 4 months (that takes 6-9 months realistically)
- Generate enough leads to attract a contractor (need to be at 30-50/mo)
- Replace the value of a tenant contractor's actual GBP and real reviews

**The real moat-building work** is when you sign a tenant contractor whose GBP, real reviews, and local trust signals get linked to your site. Citations are the floor that holds the building up. The walls go up when you have a partner.
