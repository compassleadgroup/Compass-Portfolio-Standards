## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

This is a reference playbook, not a script to read aloud. Read it once cover to cover. Then keep it open during your three-call sequence with each contractor. Sections 3, 4, 7, and 10 are the most consulted in real time.

**For Tulsa specifically:** Sections 12 and 13 contain your direct application. Everything else is generalized for future sites.

**Order of execution per deal:**

## PART 1: PRE-CLOSE ASSET CHECKLIST
### What Your Site Must Have Before Any Contractor Pitch

You cannot close a serious contractor until you've proven the asset works. Pitching too early kills deals. Pitching too late costs revenue. This checklist tells you when you're ready.

### 1.1 Minimum Asset Maturity

| Asset | Threshold | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Domain age | 3+ months indexed | Past sandbox, trust signal |
| Page count | 40 to 200 pages | Sterling Sky safe, not spammy |
| Search Console impressions | 200+ over 14 days | Proof of organic discovery |
| Primary keyword ranking | Page 2 minimum | Shows real momentum |
| Longtail page-1 rankings | 5+ keywords | Conversion-ready traffic |
| CallRail history | 30+ days live | Real call data |
| Billable calls | 5+ per month minimum | Demonstrable lead generation |
| Form submissions | 3+ per week | Multi-channel proof |
| TCPA consent block | On every form | Legal defensibility |
| Header disclosure strip | Present site-wide | FTC compliance, deal-killer if missing |
| Citations live | 20+ minimum | Local SEO credibility |
| Schema markup | LocalBusiness + Service + FAQPage | Signals legitimacy |

### 1.2 The 14-Day Lead Floor (By Niche)

You need this many qualified leads in the trailing 14 days before pitching flat rent:

| Niche | Min calls / 14 days | Acceptable for PPL trial |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation repair | 3-5 | 1+ |
| Plumbing, HVAC, electrical | 8-15 | 3+ |
| Roofing | 5-8 | 2+ |
| Water damage / restoration | 4-6 | 1+ |
| Tree service, garage door | 8-12 | 3+ |
| Solar, generator, EV charger | 3-5 | 1+ |
| Pest control, mold | 6-10 | 2+ |
| Personal injury law | 2-4 signed-intent | 1+ |

**Why 14 days, not 30:** Contractors discount month-old data. They think in two-week pipeline windows. A 14-day sample showing 3 foundation calls is more persuasive than a 30-day sample showing the same 3 calls, because it implies a 6+ calls/month run rate.

### 1.3 Proof Package (Pre-Built, Screen-Shareable)

Before booking Call 1, have a single Google Drive folder ready containing:

1. **Lead recordings.** 3 to 5 best calls. Trim to intent statement ("we have cracks in our slab and need someone to look at it"). Redact last names and addresses. CallRail makes this easy.
2. **Ranking screenshots.** 4 to 6 money keywords, date-stamped, incognito + Tulsa VPN.
3. **Search Console export.** 14-day impressions, clicks, average position trending up.
4. **Call log.** Date, time, duration, intent classification, disposition. Spreadsheet format.
5. **Qualified-lead definition (one page).** This becomes a clause in your term sheet verbatim.
6. **Geographic heatmap.** CallRail area code overlay or Analytics city data showing leads concentrated in target geo.
7. **Competitor lead-cost comparison.** What Angi, HomeAdvisor, LSA, and Service Direct charge per lead in this niche vs. your effective per-lead cost at the rent you'll ask.

### 1.4 Operator-Side Pre-Pitch Readiness

You cannot close a contractor on Tuesday if your back-end isn't ready by Monday. Before Call 1, verify all of these exist:

- [ ] CallRail tracking number with whisper message and recording enabled
- [ ] Forwarding destination flippable in under 60 seconds
- [ ] Stripe or GoCardless billing ready for ACH/card authorization
- [ ] Term sheet PDF pre-filled with all clauses except price, geo, start date
- [ ] DocuSign or PandaDoc loaded with the template
- [ ] Lead dispute log spreadsheet template ready
- [ ] Bank account capable of receiving ACH (some online banks have receiving holds)

If any of these are missing, you are not ready to pitch. Fix them this week.

## PART 2: CONTRACTOR QUALIFICATION FRAMEWORK

The frame is: **"I'm qualifying contractors for a limited number of slots."** That frame collapses if your back-end is sloppy or if you fold on price. Pre-qualification protects the frame.

### 2.1 The 10 Qualifying Questions (Spread Across Calls 1 and 2)

| # | Question | What to listen for | Kill threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Average ticket on a typical job? | Specific number, no hedging | "Depends" with no follow-up |
| 2 | Gross margin on that average job? | 30-60% on most home services | Below 20% |
| 3 | Out of 10 estimates, how many close? | Self-reported close rate | Below 20% on high-ticket |
| 4 | How fast does someone call back a form lead? | Under 5 minutes ideal | Over 30 minutes |
| 5 | How many crews running, calendar status? | Capacity for 5-20 new leads/month | Booked 6+ weeks solid |
| 6 | Current marketing spend on Angi/HomeAdvisor/LSA/Ads? | Specific numbers | "Nothing, just referrals" |
| 7 | Walk me through your licensing and insurance? | Trade license, GL, bond | Missing or expired |
| 8 | Who owns the decision to sign a marketing contract? | Sole decider or two-person | 3+ deciders, none on call |
| 9 | Longest tenure with a marketing vendor, and why ended? | 12+ months, reasonable cause | Churns every 3 months |
| 10 | If I sent 8 qualified calls next month, how would you handle it? | Real answer about scheduling, vans, techs | "I dunno" |

### 2.2 Contractor Scoring System (0-100)

Score before Call 3. Below 60: walk or send to premium PPL. Above 80: lock them in.

| Dimension | Weight | 0 points | 5 points | 10 points |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Avg ticket × margin (gross profit per job) | 20 | Under $300 | $300-$1,000 | Over $1,000 |
| Self-reported close rate | 15 | Under 20% | 20-40% | Over 40% |
| Response time to inbound | 15 | Over 1 hour | 5-60 min | Under 5 min |
| Current marketing spend & sophistication | 10 | None or clueless | Under $1k/mo basic | $2k+/mo articulate |
| Crew capacity headroom | 10 | 0-1 crew booked | 2 crews | 3+ crews with room |
| Licensing and insurance | 10 | Missing | Partial | Full + bond |
| Review profile | 10 | Under 4.0 stars | 4.0-4.5 | 4.6+ with volume |
| Decision-maker clarity | 5 | Unclear committee | 2-person | Sole decider on call |
| Tenure with past vendors | 5 | Churns under 3mo | 6-12 months | 12+ months |
| Operator gut-check (vibe, integrity signals) | 5 | Red flags | Neutral | Positive signals |

### 2.3 Instant Disqualifiers (Don't Even Run the Score)

- Licensing or insurance expired
- Sub-4.0 Google rating with communication complaints
- Multiple lawsuits or BBB complaints against past marketing vendors
- Asks you to skip TCPA consent
- Demands statewide exclusivity
- Demands to own the domain
- Demands 0-day termination clause
- Booked solid 6+ weeks (no capacity = no need)
- Refuses to put a card on file
- Asks for "free trial" with no payment authorization

## PART 3: THE THREE-CALL SALES SEQUENCE

This is the core of the playbook. Bushmaker's framework rests on extracting numbers across low-pressure conversations so that by the close, the math is already on the table and you're just walking them through their own data.

### 3.1 Call 1: Info Gathering / Fit (20-25 minutes)

**Objective:** Extract qualifying numbers without disclosing your offer. Book Call 2.

**Opening script:**
> "Appreciate you jumping on. Before I tell you what we do, I want to make sure this is even a fit on both sides. I've got a lead generation asset for foundation repair in Tulsa, and I'm talking to a few contractors to figure out who's the right home for it. Mind if I ask you a few questions about how you operate, and then I'll tell you what I'm sitting on?"

**Questions to ask this call (in order):** 1, 2, 5, 6, 7 from section 2.1. Save 3, 4, 8, 9, 10 for Call 2.

**Ballpark extraction language:**
> "Ballpark, when a homeowner calls and you go look, what's a typical job land at? I'm not holding you to it, just give me a range."

Repeat their answer back, write it down, reference it later. Locking in casual numbers in low-stakes calls means they cannot revise upward on Call 3.

**Closing the call (transition to Call 2):**
> "Look, I think there's a real fit here based on what you told me. Before I share lead volume and recordings, let me pull the last two weeks of call data and put together a quick view for you. Can we do 20 minutes Thursday at 2?"

**Do not** pitch numbers, share recordings, or show rankings on Call 1. Book Call 2.

### 3.2 Call 2: Social Proof + Capacity (25-30 minutes)

**Objective:** Play recordings, verify capacity, anchor close rate down, book Call 3.

**Opening script:**
> "I pulled the data. Let me share my screen. I want to play you a call from last Tuesday and one from Friday so you can hear the kind of homeowner that's coming through."

Play recording. Stop after intent statement. Ask:
> "Is that the kind of customer you want?"

Get a yes. That yes is leverage on Call 3.

**Close-rate extraction:**
> "If a homeowner like that called you and you sent your estimator out, ballpark how often does that turn into a signed job? I know it depends, just ballpark."

They'll say a high number. Foundation contractors typically claim 40-60%. Roofers 30-50%. Plumbers 50-70%. You will anchor this down on Call 3 (see section 4.3).

**Capacity verification:**
> "If five of those came in next month, plus your normal load, can your crews handle it? I don't want to send you something you can't service."

Get a specific yes ("yeah, two crews and one is light right now") or surface the capacity problem now.

**Transition to Call 3:**
> "OK. What I want to do next call is run the numbers with you live. I'll plug in your average ticket, your margin, your close rate, the volume I'm seeing, and we'll see if this makes sense for both of us. If the math doesn't work I'll tell you. Can we do 30 minutes Tuesday morning?"

You've now told them you'll run a calculator. The frame is "we'll see if this makes sense" not "I'm going to sell you."

### 3.3 Call 3: Calculator / Trial / Close (30-45 minutes)

Full structure in Section 4. The close itself is in Section 7.

## PART 4: THE ROI CALCULATOR

This is the load-bearing artifact of the close. Build it in Google Sheets, share read-only, screen-share live.

### 4.1 Inputs to Gather Before Call 3

1. Projected monthly leads (from your data, conservative)
2. Self-reported close rate (from Call 2)
3. Anchored close rate (set live on Call 3, see 4.3)
4. Average ticket (from Call 1, locked in)
5. Gross margin % (from Call 1)
6. Your rent ask (from formula in section 5.1)

### 4.2 The Math (Visible to the Contractor)

```
Monthly Leads × Anchored Close Rate × Avg Ticket = Projected Revenue
Projected Revenue × Gross Margin % = Projected Gross Profit
Projected Gross Profit − Monthly Rent = Net Profit to Contractor
Net Profit ÷ Monthly Rent = ROI Multiple
```

Pricing formula (yours, set off-screen):
```
Monthly Rent = Monthly Leads × Anchored Close Rate × Avg Ticket × Margin × 10%
```

This produces a rent delivering ~10x ROI to the contractor at conservative inputs.

### 4.3 Anchored Close Rate by Niche (Critical)

Contractors self-report close rates including warm referrals. Internet leads close at materially lower rates.

| Niche | Self-reported avg | Anchor for calculator |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation repair | 40-60% | 20-25% |
| Roofing (replacement) | 30-50% | 15-20% |
| HVAC (repair) | 40-70% | 25-35% |
| HVAC (install) | 25-40% | 15-20% |
| Plumbing (emergency) | 50-80% | 35-45% |
| Plumbing (project) | 30-50% | 20-25% |
| Water damage / restoration | 50-80% | 35-50% |
| Tree service | 40-85% | 30-40% |
| Solar | 8-20% (exclusive) | 5-10% |
| Electrical (panel) | 40-60% | 25-35% |
| Garage door | 45-70% | 30-45% |
| Pest control | 50-70% | 30-45% |
| Mold remediation | 40-60% | 25-35% |
| EV charger install | 30-50% | 15-25% |
| Generator install | 25-45% | 15-25% |

**The anchor script:**
> "You said you close 50% in the field. I believe you, on referrals and warm intros that's a real number. Internet leads are different. They're shopping. Industry benchmarks on inbound digital leads run materially lower. For the math today I want to use a number that's defensible if a homeowner calls and you don't book them on the first try. Let's use 25% for foundation. If you beat it, you make more. Fair?"

They'll agree because you've shown your work.

### 4.4 Visual Presentation

- Share the screen, not a PDF. Live editing implies transparency.
- Highlight Net Profit and ROI Multiple in large green font.
- Show three scenarios in three columns: Conservative (your anchored rate), Realistic (anchored + 5pts), Stretch (their self-reported rate). Eye goes to the middle column.
- Do not say "ROI." Say "for every dollar you pay me, you put $X back in your pocket after costs."
- Silence works. Let them stare at the math.

### 4.5 The Calculator Pitch (Verbatim)

> "OK, screen-share is up. Let me plug in what we talked about. You said average ticket $7,500. You said margin around 45%. We agreed on a 25% close rate for the math. I'm seeing about 6 qualified phone calls a month from the asset right now, but I want to be conservative so let's use 5.
>
> So: 5 calls × 25% × $7,500 = $9,375 in revenue. At 45% margin, that's $4,219 in gross profit. The rent on this asset is $850 a month. So you net $3,369 a month, or just under five-to-one on every dollar you pay me. That's the conservative column.
>
> If you close at 30% instead of 25%, the net is $5,200. If your average ticket is closer to $9,000 like you mentioned for piering jobs, the net jumps again.
>
> Does that math make sense to you?"

Stop talking. Whoever speaks first loses.

## PART 5: PRICING MODEL DECISION TREE

### 5.1 Flat Monthly Rent

**Use when:** High-trust contractor who has run paid marketing before; niche with high ticket and steady volume (foundation, roofing, HVAC install, water damage); you want predictable billing; 14-day proof shows 4+ qualified leads with low variance.

**Formula:**
```
Monthly Rent = (Conservative Monthly Leads × Anchored Close Rate × Avg Ticket × Gross Margin) × 10%
```

**Tulsa foundation worked example:** 5 × 25% × $7,500 × 45% × 10% = $422. Round up to $500 floor or $850 with strong proof package.

**Flat rent ranges (2025-2026 market):**

| Niche | Low | Mid | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation repair | $500 | $900 | $1,800 |
| Roofing | $700 | $1,500 | $3,500 |
| HVAC | $400 | $1,000 | $2,500 |
| Plumbing | $400 | $900 | $2,000 |
| Water damage / restoration | $800 | $1,800 | $4,000 |
| Tree service | $300 | $700 | $1,500 |
| Solar | $800 | $2,000 | $5,000+ |
| Electrical | $300 | $700 | $1,500 |
| Garage door | $300 | $600 | $1,200 |
| Pest control | $250 | $500 | $1,000 |
| Mold remediation | $500 | $1,200 | $2,500 |
| EV charger | $300 | $600 | $1,200 |
| Generator install | $500 | $1,200 | $2,500 |
| Spray foam | $500 | $1,000 | $2,200 |
| Crawl space encapsulation | $500 | $1,100 | $2,400 |

### 5.2 Pay-Per-Lead (PPL)

**Use when:** First-time tenant; skeptical contractor; high lead-quality variance niches (legal, solar, restoration); you plan to convert them to flat rent later.

**Pricing benchmarks (2025-2026):**

| Niche | Form lead | Phone (qualified) | Exclusive phone (premium) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation repair | $40 | $90 | $150 |
| Roofing | $50 | $120 | $200-$350 |
| HVAC | $30 | $80 | $150-$250 |
| Plumbing | $20 | $60 | $100-$150 |
| Water damage / mold | $75 | $200 | $300-$700 |
| Tree service | $20 | $50 | $75-$100 |
| Solar | $50 | $150 | $200-$300 (appt) |
| Electrical | $25 | $60 | $100-$165 |
| Garage door | $20 | $45 | $70 |
| Pest control | $20 | $50 | $80 |
| EV charger | $25 | $60 | $100 |
| Generator | $40 | $100 | $150-$200 |
| Spray foam | $30 | $75 | $125 |
| Crawl space | $40 | $90 | $150 |

**Rule of thumb:** Charge 5-10% of expected revenue per closed job. Foundation: $7,500 avg ticket × 25% close = $1,875 revenue per lead. 5% = $94. That's your phone-call price.

### 5.3 Hybrid (Base + Per-Lead)

**Use when:** High-ticket low-volume niches (foundation, solar, generator, restoration); you want predictable revenue + upside; contractor wants insurance against a slow month.

**Structure:**
- Base = 50-60% of flat-rent equivalent ($500 base instead of $850 flat)
- Per-lead = $50-$150 above base
- Cap monthly total at 1.5x flat-rent equivalent

### 5.4 Revenue Share

**Use when:** Phase 2 relationship after 6-12 months of flat rent. Required: CRM read-only access in writing, airtight call attribution.

**Typical structure:** 5-10% of closed-job revenue, monthly reconciliation, right-to-audit clause.

**NEVER use on Day 1.** Tenant under-reports closes when there's no enforcement mechanism.

### 5.5 Decision Tree

```
Is this their first time buying inbound digital leads?
├── YES → Pay-per-lead with $X cap, convert to flat after 60 days
└── NO
    ├── Avg ticket >$5,000 AND volume <8/mo → Hybrid (base + per-lead)
    ├── Avg ticket >$10,000 AND CRM access agreed → Rev share 7-10% (relationship-tested only)
    ├── Steady volume, sophisticated contractor → Flat monthly rent
    └── Lead quality variance high → Pay-per-lead
```

## PART 6: TRIAL OFFER STRUCTURES

The trial is your highest-leverage closing tool. James Dooley's "Leads First" methodology uses no-win-no-fee structures. Adapt for the trial.

### 6.1 The Free-Lead Trial (Dooley-Style)

**Structure:**
- 2 to 4 free qualified leads, capped at 7 days from start
- "Qualified" = inbound call >90 seconds OR form submission with valid name, phone, service intent
- After cap or 7 days (whichever first), meter starts
- Contract is signed at the start of trial, payment auth captured

**Script:**
> "Here's what I'll do. I'll give you the next 3 qualified calls free. You answer them, run the appointments, tell me what you think. After call #3 or 7 days, whichever hits first, the contract kicks in at $850/month. You can cancel before the meter starts. Worst case for you: you got 3 free leads."

### 6.2 Pay-Only-For-Qualified-Calls First 30 Days

- Month 1 is PPL at $X per qualified call, capped at flat-rent equivalent
- Month 2 auto-converts to flat rent unless written opt-out 7 days before month-end

### 6.3 The Guarantee That "Scares You a Little Bit"

Bound it. Do not write unbounded guarantees.

**Operator-friendly guarantees:**
- "Money-back if leads under X in month 1" where X is half your conservative projection
- "Replace any unqualified lead within 48 hours of dispute" (narrowly defined)
- "If 90 days in you're not netting 3x rent, I'll renegotiate" (soft, signals confidence)

**Never offer:**
- ROI guarantees ("I guarantee you'll make $X")
- "Free until you close a job" (moral hazard)
- Open-ended refunds

### 6.4 Trial-to-Paid Conversion

- Trial must end with automatic conversion to paid unless written opt-out. Defaults win.
- Day 5 check-in: "How are the calls going? Anything to adjust before the meter starts Friday?"
- Capture signed payment authorization at start of trial, not end.

This is a term sheet, not contract language. It captures deal points an attorney will turn into a contract. Negotiation notes are inline.

### LEAD GENERATION SERVICES AGREEMENT — TERM SHEET

**Effective Date:** [Date]
**Asset:** tulsafoundationexperts.com and associated tracking number, GBP, content

---

#### Clause A. Parties & Scope

**Default:** Operator grants Tenant non-exclusive, non-transferable access to inbound leads generated by the Asset during the Term. Operator is an independent contractor. This is not a partnership, joint venture, or employer relationship.

**Negotiation notes:** Tenants ask for "exclusive partnership" language. Refuse. Use "engagement" or "lead-routing services." Avoid any language that creates fiduciary duty.

---

#### Clause B. Lead Definition (Billable Lead Criteria)

**Default:** A "Qualified Lead" means any of:
1. Phone call ≥90 seconds where caller identifies a service need within the niche AND is located within Service Area
2. Form submission with valid name, valid US phone number (no VOIP duplicates), and stated service need
3. Text inquiry to tracked number with stated service need

**NOT a Qualified Lead:**
- Wrong-number calls
- Solicitations TO the tenant
- Existing customer calls (defined as a customer Tenant has invoiced in prior 12 months, Tenant bears burden of proof)
- Calls <90 seconds with no service intent
- Calls outside Service Area
- Duplicate calls from same number within 30 days

**Negotiation notes:** Tenants push for 3-5 minute minimum duration. Concede to 120 seconds maximum. Tenants push to exclude "tire-kicker" calls subjectively. Refuse. Define objectively or you'll dispute every lead.

---

#### Clause C. Pricing Model

**Default (Tulsa foundation):** Flat monthly rent of $850, billed monthly in advance, first month due on Effective Date.

**Alternative structures (check one):**
- [ ] Flat Rent: $______/month
- [ ] Pay-Per-Lead: $______ per Qualified Phone Call, $______ per Qualified Form Lead, billed weekly
- [ ] Hybrid: $______ base + $______ per Qualified Lead, monthly cap $______
- [ ] Revenue Share: ____% of revenue per closed job, billed monthly with CRM reconciliation

**Negotiation notes:** Volume discount at flat rent is OK (they pay same regardless of volume). Revenue share requires CRM read-only access in writing or it's unenforceable.

---

#### Clause D. Payment Terms

**Default:**
- First month due in full on Effective Date via ACH or credit card on file
- Subsequent months auto-debited on the 1st
- Late payment >5 days: 1.5%/month interest + suspension of lead forwarding on day 7
- Returned payment: $35 fee + immediate suspension
- Cancellation requires 30 days written notice

**Negotiation notes:** Tenants ask for net-30 or arrears. Refuse on first deals. After 6 months of on-time payments, extend to net-15. Never net-30. You are not their bank.

---

#### Clause E. Exclusivity

**Default:** Operator grants Tenant exclusivity for [Service Category: foundation repair, slab repair, pier installation, crawl space, drainage] within Service Area [Tulsa County] for the Term. Exclusivity does NOT extend to:
- Adjacent counties (Operator may operate separate assets in Wagoner, Rogers, Osage)
- Adjacent service categories (basement waterproofing, concrete leveling) unless explicitly listed
- Other Operator assets ranking for different keywords or markets

**Remedy for breach:** If Operator routes leads elsewhere in violation, Tenant gets pro-rata rent abatement for affected days plus right to terminate with 7 days notice.

**Negotiation notes:** Tenants want statewide exclusivity. Refuse. Define narrowly by city/metro + service category with explicit remedies.

---

#### Clause F. Trial Period

**Default:** First 7 calendar days OR 3 Qualified Leads (whichever first) are no-charge. Tenant must answer leads in good faith. Trial converts to paid term on day 8 or after 3rd Qualified Lead unless Tenant gives written cancellation notice before trigger.

**Negotiation notes:** Tenants ask for 30-day free trials. Refuse. You'll burn 30 days of leads. The cap on lead count (3) is your protection.

---

#### Clause G. Term & Termination

**Default:**
- Initial Term: 6 months from Effective Date
- Renewal: Auto-renews month-to-month after Initial Term unless either party gives 30 days written notice
- Termination for Cause (immediate, either party):
  - Material breach uncured 10 days after written notice
  - Non-payment >15 days
  - Bankruptcy/insolvency
  - Loss of trade license or insurance
  - Tenant lawsuit against Operator
- Termination Without Cause: 30 days written notice after Initial Term. During Initial Term, no termination without cause; Tenant remains liable for balance of 6 months.

**Negotiation notes:** Tenants fight 6-month lockup. Hold the line on first deals. Acceptable concession: 90-day initial term with 60-day notice. Add an "out clause" for documented ranking collapse (loss of top-3 ranking for primary keyword >21 consecutive days outside verified algo update).

---

#### Clause H. Lead Dispute Process

**Default:**
- 48 hours from lead delivery to dispute in writing
- Dispute must include: lead ID, timestamp, specific reason
- Operator reviews and responds within 72 hours
- If Operator agrees: lead is credited (PPL) or removed from monthly count (flat rent, does not reduce rent but is documented)
- Dispute cap: No more than 20% of monthly leads may be disputed
- Disputes not raised within 48 hours are waived

**Negotiation notes:** Tenants ask for 7-day windows and unlimited disputes. Refuse. 48 hours and 20% cap is operator-friendly. Hold it.

---

#### Clause I. Volume Commitments

**Default:** Operator makes NO guarantee of lead volume. Commercially reasonable efforts only. Non-breach events:
- Google algorithm updates (verifiable via Google or Search Engine Land)
- GBP suspension or policy change
- Acts of God, weather, internet outages
- Competitor SEO actions
- Tenant's brand reputation issues affecting CTR

**Tenant's extended-underperformance exit:** If Qualified Lead volume drops below 50% of trailing 90-day average for 30+ consecutive days outside documented force majeure, Tenant may terminate with 14 days notice.

**Negotiation notes:** Refuse guaranteed lead minimums. Concede the underperformance exit ramp and that's it.

---

#### Clause J. Operator Ownership

**Default:** Operator is and remains the sole owner of:
- The Domain
- All website content, code, copy
- The Google Business Profile (if applicable)
- All tracking phone numbers
- All call recordings
- All form submission data
- All historical lead data
- All backlinks pointing to Asset
- The brand name and any associated marks

**Tenant acknowledges:** None of the above is licensed, transferred, or available for purchase. Tenant has no claim on termination.

**Negotiation notes:** This is the spine of rank-and-rent. NEVER compromise. If tenant insists on owning domain or GBP, walk.

---

#### Clause K. License Grant to Tenant

**Default:** Operator grants Tenant a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license during the Term to:
1. Receive forwarded phone calls from tracking number
2. Receive form submissions and lead notifications
3. Display Tenant's business name on website ONLY in operator-controlled tenant-display modules (e.g., "Powered by [Tenant]" footer)

**Tenant does NOT receive:** login credentials, ability to modify content, rights to use the domain in marketing materials, any rights post-termination.

**Negotiation notes:** Acceptable concession: a tenant-name disclosure on contact page. Not acceptable: their logo in the header.

---

#### Clause L. Tenant Obligations

**Default:**
1. Answer Qualified Lead phone calls within 5 minutes during business hours (7am-7pm local, M-Sat) and within 30 minutes after hours
2. Respond to form leads within 15 minutes during business hours
3. Maintain all licenses, insurance, and bonds throughout Term
4. Provide service in good faith and at industry-standard quality
5. NOT solicit leads off-platform (not ask homeowner to "next time call us directly" before Term + 24 months non-circumvent)
6. Provide Operator with monthly close-rate data (approximate) for ROI tracking
7. Notify Operator within 24 hours of any complaint, lawsuit, or licensing action

**Negotiation notes:** Hold the 5-minute rule. MIT Lead Response Study shows contractors responding within 5 minutes were 21x more likely to qualify a lead than those responding in 30 minutes. If they can't hit 5 minutes during business hours, they'll burn the leads and blame you.

---

#### Clause M. Non-Circumvent / Non-Solicitation

**Default:** During Term and for 24 months after termination, Tenant shall not:
1. Contact any homeowner introduced via Asset for services competing with the niche, EXCEPT continued service of an existing job started during the Term
2. Solicit Operator to terminate the relationship and serve Tenant outside the Agreement
3. Reproduce or reverse-engineer the Asset (domain naming, content structure) to replicate lead flow in Service Area

**Liquidated damages:** $1,500 per lead converted off-platform OR 3x average ticket of converted job, whichever is greater, plus enforcement costs.

**Negotiation notes:** 18 months acceptable. 12 months is the floor. Anchor liquidated damages at 3x ticket. Courts uphold liquidated damages that approximate actual harm.

**Negotiation notes:** Mutual indemnification is standard. Resist unlimited operator indemnity. Cap your indemnity at 12 months of rent. Tenant's indemnity to you should be uncapped, they have malpractice exposure.

## PART 9: RED FLAGS AND WALK-AWAY TRIGGERS

### 9.1 Contractor Red Flags (Kill the Deal)

- License or insurance missing/expired
- Sub-4.0 stars on Google with communication complaints
- Multiple lawsuits or BBB complaints against past marketing vendors
- Will not put a card on file
- Can't articulate average ticket, margin, or close rate within 10 seconds
- Booked solid 6+ weeks
- Demands statewide exclusivity, domain ownership, or 0-day termination
- Asks you to do anything that smells like TCPA exposure
- Wants contract reviewed by attorney for >14 days with no specific edits

### 9.2 Operator-Side Warning Signs Mid-Pitch

- You're discounting from $850 to $400 just to close (wrong contractor)
- You catch yourself overpromising volume or close rate
- More than 90 minutes total on Calls 1+2 (they're free-consulting you)
- They keep saying "yes but" to every reframe
- They want to "test" you with a non-binding trial without payment auth

### 9.3 When to Take Less Money for a Better Contractor

**Take less when:**
- 4.9 stars, 200+ reviews, 24-month tenure with last vendor
- Crew capacity headroom (2+ crews not booked)
- They've referred others to you
- High-LTV niche (foundation, solar, generator)
- Flat-rent commitment in a niche where churn replacement is painful

**Hold the line when:**
- Tenant has worked with multiple lead-gen vendors and respects the model
- High-volume niches (HVAC, plumbing) where you can re-rent in 7 days
- Contractor operating multiple locations and the deal sets pricing precedent

### 9.4 The 24-Hour Rule (Before Signing)

If unsure whether to sign:
- Have I run a license/insurance check?
- Have I read their Google reviews?
- Have I sanity-checked their self-reported numbers against industry benchmarks?
- Have I priced this on the formula or on hope?
- Would I still do this deal at 80% of the price?

Any "no" or "I don't know" = do homework before signing. Bad tenants cost more than empty assets.

## PART 11: SALES PSYCHOLOGY & FRAME CONTROL

### 11.1 The Frame Inversion

Default contractor expectation: marketer is selling, contractor is buying.
Your frame: I have an asset producing leads. I'm choosing which contractor gets it.

Language that reinforces the inversion:
- "I'm talking to a few contractors in Tulsa to figure out who's the right home for this."
- "Before I share the numbers, I want to make sure your operation can handle the volume."
- "If this isn't a fit, I'd rather know now."
- "I'm not interested in working with someone who's going to fumble the calls. I've had that happen."

### 11.2 Status Anchoring (Use Only When True)

- "I've got two other foundation contractors who've asked about this. I'd rather work with you because of [specific reason], but I need to know by Friday."

Lying about other interest torches your reputation in small contractor markets. Only use when genuine.

### 11.3 The Silence Move

After delivering the calculator math:
> "Does that math make sense to you?"

Stop talking. Count to 30 silently. The first one to speak loses the negotiation. Most contractors will fill the silence by either (a) asking a clarifying question (signals interest) or (b) trying to negotiate price (signals close).

### 11.4 The Pre-Emptive Concession

Before they ask, give a small concession to anchor the conversation around concessions you control:
> "Look, I know $850 is on the higher end. What I'll do is give you 3 free leads first so you're not betting anything before you see the quality."

You appear flexible while having conceded nothing on price.

### 11.5 Showing Your Work

Contractors trust numbers more than pitches. Every claim should be backed:
- "I'm projecting 5 leads because that's the low end of the last 14 days, not the average."
- "I'm using 25% close rate because the MIT study shows internet leads convert at one-third the rate of referrals."
- "I'm pricing at $850 because that's 10% of your projected gross profit at conservative inputs."

The math is the salesperson. You're just the analyst.

## PART 12: THE BUSHMAKER CLOSE (FULL SCRIPT)

After the calculator pitch and silence:

> "Look, based on what we just walked through, the math is in your favor by about 5 to 1. The asset is producing now. I've shown you the calls. You've told me your team can handle the volume.
>
> The next step is I send you the term sheet, you sign it, and I forward the next 3 qualified calls free as your trial. After that, $850 a month auto-debits on the 1st.
>
> The two questions you have to answer for me are:
> One, do you want this.
> Two, is there anything missing for you to say yes today.
>
> Because if it's a yes, I want to get the calls flowing this week."

Stop. Whoever speaks first loses.

### 12.1 If Yes
> "Perfect. I'm sending the term sheet to your email right now. Sign it, I'll send the payment auth link, and we go live within 24 hours. While we're on the phone, what's the best number to forward calls to?"

Cement immediately. Don't wait for tomorrow.

### 12.2 If Maybe / Need to Think
> "Totally fair. Three options for what's behind that. Money, math, or another decision-maker. Which one?"

Handle whichever they name. Always book the follow-up before hanging up (max 5 business days out).

### 12.3 If No
> "Got it. Two things before we hang up. One, what was the dealbreaker? I want to know so I don't pitch the next contractor wrong. Two, if I cold-call you in 6 months with twice this lead volume, are we having a different conversation?"

Get the data. Keep the door open. Move on.

## PART 15: COMMON MISTAKES AND HOW TO AVOID THEM

### 15.1 Pricing on Hope, Not Formula
Mistake: "I think $1,500/mo sounds good." 
Fix: Use the formula every time. Monthly Leads × Anchored Close Rate × Avg Ticket × Margin × 10%.

### 15.2 Pitching Too Early
Mistake: Pitching at week 3 with no lead data.
Fix: Wait until you have 14 consecutive days of qualified leads at the floor for your niche.

### 15.3 Discounting to Close
Mistake: Dropping from $850 to $400 when they push back.
Fix: If they won't pay your number, they're not the right tenant. Walk and find one who will.

### 15.4 Skipping the Three-Call Sequence
Mistake: Trying to close on Call 1.
Fix: Calls 1 and 2 exist to extract numbers and lock them in. Call 3 is the close.

### 15.5 No TCPA Consent on Forms
Mistake: Generic contact form with no consent language.
Fix: Add consent string above submit button. Name the tenant business in the disclosure.

### 15.6 Forgetting the Non-Circumvent Clause
Mistake: Tenant churns at month 8, continues servicing your homeowners.
Fix: 24-month non-circumvent with $1,500/lead OR 3x ticket liquidated damages.

### 15.7 Not Capturing Payment Auth Before Trial Starts
Mistake: "We'll start billing after the trial."
Fix: Card on file at trial start. Trial auto-converts to paid unless written opt-out.

### 15.8 Letting Tenant Own Anything
Mistake: "Sure, you can have admin access to the website."
Fix: NO. Operator owns domain, content, GBP, phone number, leads. Tenant has a license, not equity.

### 15.9 Unbounded Guarantees
Mistake: "I'll guarantee you 10 leads a month or full refund."
Fix: Define what you can control (lead quality criteria, ranking force majeure). Never guarantee revenue or volume.

### 15.10 Talking After the Math
Mistake: Filling silence after the calculator with more pitch.
Fix: Ask "does this math make sense to you" and stop. Whoever speaks first loses.

### 15.11 Sending a Proposal Instead of Closing on the Call
Mistake: "I'll email you a proposal."
Fix: Screen-share the term sheet live. Send for e-signature immediately after the call.

### 15.12 Not Booking the Follow-Up
Mistake: "Let me know what you decide."
Fix: "Let's put 20 minutes on the calendar Thursday for a clean yes or no."
